About Me

ZACHARY JAMES SILVA

Where I'm from

I was born and raised in Albuquerque, New Mexico. I graduated from La Cueva High School in 2013 with Honors. I had the privilege to play baseball and football for La Cueva all four years of high school. Since I left to college, my parents have lived in Philadelphia and currently Colorado Springs.

What I do

I am on the football team at Rose-Hulman and am a two-year letter winner so far. I also enjoy playing basketball and going for long runs. Outside of the classroom you can find me either working out or reading a book. My workouts are a key part of my day and relieve lots of built up stress in the busy life of an engineering student. My favorite books are self-improvement and business motivation. Books allow me to gain more knowledge on how I can be a better and more productive person.

Where I want to be

My goal after graduation is to work as an engineer for an innovative company. I am interested in RF/Microwave Engineering and also Digital/FPGA Design. Eventually, I would like to further my education with either an MSEE or MBA. I want to do work that will make an impact and ultimately make the world a better and safer place.

Experience

Here are my current and past technical experiences.

  • 2013 - Present

    Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology

    Terre Haute, Indiana

    I am a senior electrical engineer at Rose-Hulman. I have taken higher level courses in electromagnetics where I have gained experience designing and simulating in CST, and testing different structures with the VNA. I have utilized Matlab in my communications and signal processing courses.

  • Summer 2014

    Bridgers & Paxton Consulting

    Albuquerque, New Mexico

    As an electrical engineering intern at B&P I assisted in various power calculation. I gained experience working directly with customers and delivering documents in a timely manner. This position enhanced my skills in AutoCad and Revit softwares with the electrical design suite. Some projects I worked on were for Sandia and Los Alamos National Labs. I gained experience working with proprietary information and how to handle secure documents.

  • Summer 2015

    General Electric Aviation

    Jacksonville, Florida

    As a Digital Engineering/Technology Intern I was able to develop FPGA logic to be used on a prototype engine exciter board. I coded mainly in Verilog HDL and also designed the board in ORCAD. This job also required project documentation and development of circuit test specifications. Along with design, I was able to work in manufacturing components and circuit assemlblies.

  • Summer 2016

    Sandia National Laboratories

    Albuquerque, New Mexico

    FGPA formal methods verification research and development
